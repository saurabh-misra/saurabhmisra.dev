I'm Saurabh Misra, a senior software engineer based in Mumbai, India, working remotely with US-based clients and teams.

<img src="/me-small.jpg" width="400px" className="about-me-page-pic" />

Most of my career — over 13 years of it — has been spent on large, legacy ColdFusion codebases: the kind that quietly run critical operations for years and need a senior engineer who can maintain them, extend them, integrate new systems with them, or modernize them gradually. I've typically worked as the sole developer or as part of a small team of senior engineers, with each of us owning our own projects end-to-end.

## Current availability

I'm currently open to senior full-time remote roles and freelance contract work, with a particular focus on ColdFusion and CFML-adjacent engagements — maintenance, modernization, REST API design, SQL Server work, and integration projects.

## The work I'm proudest of

For a US-based healthcare client, I single-handedly designed and built the API layer powering their new e-commerce platform, including a custom integration with their Sage ERP, payment processing via Paya, and inventory and shipping logistics. Within three years the platform was generating roughly $2M/year in revenue, replacing a phone-only sales process that had been the company's primary channel for years.

For the same client, I also built a private, mobile-friendly social media platform exclusively for their customers — originally in ColdFusion, SQL Server, and Bootstrap, and later rebuilt as a React/Vite PWA backed by a ColdBox REST API. 

Along the way, I also single-handedly shipped a 100+ field, 16-section medical onboarding system with admin review workflows, and a REST API suite that helped migrate a monolithic Coldfusion web application into a layered application architecture with a Wordpress powered front-end.

## How I work

I've spent most of my career working remotely with US-based teams, which means I'm comfortable with asynchronous communication, written-first documentation, and the kind of independent ownership that remote engineering actually requires. I think the best engineering work comes from understanding the business context first — what the system is for, who depends on it, what failure modes are tolerable — and then choosing the simplest design that fits.

## Beyond ColdFusion

While CFML is my deepest specialty, I've also shipped production work in Node.js, Next.js, Fastify, React, Vite, Flutter/Dart, AWS (Cognito, S3), and Docker. I'm fluent with AI-augmented development workflows — particularly Claude Code, which I've used extensively to work across stacks and accelerate delivery. This range is most useful for teams who want to keep an existing ColdFusion system stable while building new services alongside it, or who are planning a gradual migration off CF and want someone who can credibly work on both sides.

## Before the freelance years

I co-founded CodeSphere Solutions, a small software development firm, where I led the business and technical direction on client projects — bringing in new clients, scoping and pricing engagements, working directly with clients on prototypes and feedback, hiring and managing junior developers, and owning architecture, design, and code review on every project before it went to production. Before that, I worked for 3 years at Infosys as part of a large team on enterprise client projects.

## Outside of work

When I'm not on client work, I write on [this blog](https://www.saurabhmisra.dev/blog/) about engineering problems I've recently worked through — usually CFML, REST API design, SQL Server, or whichever modern stack I'm currently using. Writing things up is how I think them through properly.
